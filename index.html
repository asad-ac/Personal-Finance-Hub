Credit Basics

What is Credit?

Credit is like borrowing a toy and promising to return it. If you always return it on time, people trust you and let you borrow more.

What is the scale of a credit score?

Credit scores range from 300-850 and there are different types of scores, lenders tend to use FICO scores.

What makes up a credit score?

  • Payment history - 35%
  • Amount owed - 30%
  • Length of credit history - 15%
  • New credit - 10%
  • Credit mix - 10%

Myth vs Fact

Myth: Keep Your Credit Utilization under 30%
  • Paying your statement balance by the due date keeps your credit in good standing.
  • Avoid paying near your credit limit consistently, it can affect lenders' willingness to offer you credit and make you appear risky.
  • If you pay one statment balance below 30%, it automatically maxes you're credit score as if you were well below 30% the previous months. Credit is a snapshot.
Myth: Credit cards are bad
  • Credit cards help establish a history of reliability to lenders.
  • A strong credit history is important when applying for things like mortgages or new credit cards.
  • Having an established credit history can help you qualify for better interest rates.
Myth: Pay off your balance right away
  • Pay your statement balance in full by the due date to have it reported to credit bureaus.
  • Paying off your card immediately after each use may result in no activity being reported.
  • Waiting until the statement balance posts and paying it off maximizes your credit score.
  • Only the statement balance is required by the due date; other charges roll over to the next billing cycle.

Choosing the Right Credit Card

Cash Back Cards
  • Usually can cash out the percentage of each transaction back into your debit account.
  • Banks often offer cash-back deals through their platforms from different businesses. For example, their could be one that can enabled for SeatGeek that is 5% cash-back on BofA.
  • Many cash-back cards have no annual fees making it feasible for many.
Travel Point Cards
  • Will not deposit into bank account typically, but will get points towards free hotels or flights.
  • Each bank tends to have different partners with hotels and flights. Meaning more research is required but it can have a higher ceiling than a cash back card depending on the travel card.
  • Most travel cards have annual fees, particularly those that can offer more travel points and are theoretically reserved for those with extensive credit history and being able to afford it.
